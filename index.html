Background

I transferred to UC Berkeley to study at the Haas School of Business. In my first-semester, I took the famous "Data 8" and decided I wanted to challenge myself, so I took on another major as a junior. I completed my dual bachelors degrees: B.A. in Data Science & B.S. in Business Administration within a span of 2.5 years!

In addition to my studies, I joined Formula Electric at Berkeley (FEB). FEB is UC Berkeley's Formula SAE racing team - a team of Elecrical, Mechanical, & Autonomous engineers building a fully functioning electric race car from scratch! Within 5 months I was elected to recruit and lead a team of my own. During my tenure as the team lead, we successfully raised funds to build out our $57K+ engineering project & compete in Formula Society of Automotive Engineers. To make this happen, I have worked with a range of Engineering, Strategy, & PR teams at companies including:Tesla, Rivian, Boeing, Siemens, Cadence, & Brembo.

When I'm not studying or building a race car... I'm still always learning! I've found my passion in Exploratory Data Analysis! Here I've highlighted some of my favorite projects in my portfolio. Thank you for taking a look :)

The Haas Transportation & Mobility Club

While studying at business school, I made a point to branch out to a more experienced community. It is not typically common for undergraduates at Berkeley to to interact or work with graduate peers outside of the classroom, but, I felt there was so much to learn socially and professionally. I was able to establish my Formula Electric team's first partnership with the Haas School of Business' MBA Transportation & Mobility Club.

We were invited to their annual Mobility Summit, a conference hosting current & future mobility leaders with panels dicussing a range of subjects relevant to San Francisco's sustainable transportation technology.

Organization members incude those from: Tesla, Rivian, GM, Ford, Zoox, Cruise, Waymo, & EVgo.

The Budget of an Electric Race Car?

How do we fund our purchases? We first have to charge it on our own credit cards. No really! There is no University account card - so budgeting is crucial.

I led and managed a budget tracking the spending variances for multiple pathways for every sub-team. For example, if our Dynamics team had a dual-pathway for a suspension project assembly, I made sure we tracked the parts & part costs involved each each pathway, for the entire Dynamics team. With a summary of all teams, we were able to forecast future spending.

Strategy & Partnerships: So, How do we get that kind of money???

Landing sponsorships as a student organization is really difficult. With a bit of targeteted outreach & sleek marketing, I was able to partner with a variety of companies: from getting free access Boeing's Wind tunnel in Seattle to signing $5k contracts with a few major players in the EV & electrification space.

Project: Rivian Event Engineering Panel

Our sponsors love to get involved with premier engineering talent at UC Berkeley through our team. We offered Rivian the opportunity to have a direct & relevant audience through our ability to reach +350 students involved in EV, Space, & Robotics industries.

In October 2023, I brought an R1T on campus & hosted an engineering panel that showcased how FEB is an amazing way to work with real-world applications of automotive engineering.
