What is Business School for Anyways? Networking: Haas Transportation & Mobility Club

While at business school, I made a point to branch out to a more seasoned community. For most undergrads, it's not typically common to interact with graduate peers outside of class, and I felt there was so much to learn if we interacted more socially and professionally. I was able to establish our team's first collaboration with the Haas School of Business' MBA Transportation & Mobility Club.

We attended their annual Mobility Summit, a conference hosting current & future mobility leaders with panels dicussing a range of subjects about San Francisco's sustainable transportation technology.

The Budget of an Electric Race Car?

How do we fund our purchases? We first have to charge it on our own members' credit cards. No really! There is no University account card - so budgeting is crucial.

I led and managed a budget tracking the spending variances for multiple pathways for every sub-team. For example, if our Dynamics team had a dual-pathway for a suspension project assembly, I made sure we tracked the parts & part costs involved each each pathway, for the entire Dynamics team. With a summary of all teams, we were able to forecast future spending.
